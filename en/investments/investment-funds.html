Why invest in funds?

  • No need to be an expert. You don't have to research the markets like professionals - the fund managers take care of this for you.
  • You diversify the risk. Investment funds are composed of different assets, so you avoid putting all your eggs into one basket.
  • Tax advantages. You can transfer your investment from one fund to another, whenever you want and without any fees. You will only be subject to tax when you sell it.
  • Access your money whenever you want. You can redeem your investment at any time. If you have investments in guaranteed funds, you will have to wait for a specific period of time.

What is an investment fund?

It consists of money that is pooled by various investors and invested and managed by financial market specialists. Its main appeal is that you can obtain a higher return than by investing on your own.

To invest in a fund, all you have to do is buy one or more shares/units of the fund. This makes you a participant. Invest at any time and from very small amounts.

There is a range of different types of investment funds: sustainable investment funds (ESG funds), which follow certain environmental, social and good governance criteria to invest in companies; funds that invest in stocks (companies) in a particular sector; index funds (which replicate the composition of an index to allow you, for example, to "invest as if a part of the IBEX 35 were yours") or actively managed funds (in which a management team studies the companies where it wants to invest the participants' money.

Openbank offers more than 3,000 index and actively managed investment funds in which you can invest from one euro or less. Try using our Fund Finder tool.
